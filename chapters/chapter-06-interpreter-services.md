## Chapter 6: Interpreter Services

*Interpreter services allow attendees who are deaf, hard of hearing, or have other communication needs to fully participate in convention programming. This is one of the most complex and costly accessibility services to provide, and requires earlier planning than almost any other department function. A convention that begins planning interpreter services fewer than 6 months before the event may not be able to secure coverage.*

---

### Types of Interpreter Services

#### ASL (American Sign Language) Interpreters
ASL interpreters provide real-time sign language interpretation for deaf and hard of hearing attendees. Coverage is typically provided in the main events room as a starting point, with additional rooms added as budget and staffing allow.

#### CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation)
CART services provide live captioning displayed on a screen, beneficial for attendees who are deaf, hard of hearing, or have auditory processing difficulties. CART can serve a broader audience than ASL interpretation alone and may be worth exploring as a complement or alternative depending on budget and availability.

*Note: Specific cost and vendor information for both ASL and CART services should be researched early and documented in the convention-specific configuration. Costs vary significantly by market, coverage scope, and duration.*

---

### Lead Time Requirements

Interpreter services must be sourced and contracted well in advance. The following minimum lead times are strongly recommended:

| Service | Minimum Lead Time |
|---|---|
| ASL Interpreters | 6 months (9 months recommended) |
| CART Services | To be determined — research early |

Failure to begin sourcing within these windows significantly increases the risk of being unable to secure coverage. Budget must be confirmed before contracts can be signed. *(see Chapter 5: Budget & Resources)*

---

### Coverage Planning

When planning interpreter coverage, consider:
- Which spaces will be covered (main events room as baseline, additional rooms based on budget)
- Convention hours requiring coverage
- Interpreter scheduling, rotation, and break requirements — interpreters typically cannot work for extended periods without rest and may require rotation. Confirm specific requirements with your interpreter vendor or agency
- Number of interpreters required based on coverage scope and rotation needs

---

### Interpreter Support Volunteer

A dedicated support volunteer should be assigned to interpreter services when interpreters are present. This volunteer is responsible for ensuring interpreters have what they need to perform their work comfortably, including water, snacks, meals, and assistance with small errands. One support volunteer is generally sufficient for a small number of interpreters — the appropriate ratio should be confirmed based on the number of interpreters contracted.

The support volunteer should be provided with a small discretionary budget for interpreter comfort items. *(see Chapter 5: Budget & Resources)*

---

### When Interpreter Services Cannot Be Provided

A convention may find itself unable to provide interpreter services due to budget constraints, insufficient lead time, or vendor unavailability. In this situation:
- Be transparent with attendees about what is and is not available
- Document the gap and the reason in post-con review
- Use the experience to build the case for earlier planning and budget allocation in future years
- Explore whether any partial solutions are available — for example, a single interpreter for the main events room only, or CART as a lower-cost alternative
- Record any attendee feedback about the impact of the gap to inform future requests

*The accessibility department should never promise services it cannot deliver. Managing expectations honestly is part of serving attendees well.*

---

### Finding Interpreter Services

Interpreters can typically be sourced through:
- Local interpreter agencies
- Regional deaf community organizations
- University interpreter training programs (may offer lower cost options)
- National interpreter referral services

Begin outreach early and obtain multiple quotes where possible.

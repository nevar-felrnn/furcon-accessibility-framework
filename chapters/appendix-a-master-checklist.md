## Appendix A: Master Checklist

*This checklist consolidates the action items from all chapters into a single planning reference, organized by when each item should be addressed. Chapter references are included so you can return to the relevant section for full context.*

*Items marked **[One-time]** are setup tasks that, once resolved, do not need to be repeated annually unless circumstances change. All others are recurring tasks for each convention cycle.*

---

### One-Time Setup

These items should be resolved before or during your first convention cycle. Once completed, document the outcome and revisit only if circumstances change.

- [ ] Investigate liability and insurance requirements for mobility aid loans *(Ch. 7)*
- [ ] Establish equipment inspection checklist for each type of mobility aid *(Ch. 7)*
- [ ] Research mobility aid purchase costs and local vendor rental options *(Ch. 7)*
- [ ] Confirm storage availability and capacity between conventions *(Ch. 7)*
- [ ] Investigate and formalize shared equipment ownership arrangement between partner conventions if applicable *(Ch. 5, 7)*
- [ ] Research ASL interpreter and CART service providers in each convention's local market *(Ch. 6)*
- [ ] Research CART services and establish baseline cost estimates *(Ch. 6)*
- [ ] Formally establish the escalation agreement with Safety — define the line between safety response and accessibility support *(Ch. 3, 12)*
- [ ] Confirm ASL interpreter minimum lead time with vendors (currently estimated at 6 months) *(Ch. 3, 6)*

### Before the Planning Cycle Begins

### At Convention

- [ ] Desk setup complete and confirmed ready before badge pickup opens *(Ch. 10, 13)*
- [ ] Quiet room confirmed open and camera confirmed live with IT *(Ch. 11, 13)*
- [ ] At-con contact sheet distributed to all staff *(Ch. 12, 13)*
- [ ] Pre-con briefing held before doors open *(Ch. 13)*
- [ ] Daily briefings held at the start of each shift *(Ch. 8, 13)*
- [ ] Shift handoffs completed with full briefing each day *(Ch. 10, 13)*
- [ ] Accessibility log maintained and secured at end of each day *(Ch. 10, 13)*
- [ ] Equipment loans tracked and outstanding loans followed up *(Ch. 10, 13)*
- [ ] Open items tracked and reviewed each morning *(Ch. 13)*
- [ ] Staff welfare monitored — breaks taken, Director checking in with individuals *(Ch. 13)*
- [ ] All equipment accounted for and inventoried at convention close *(Ch. 13)*
- [ ] Quiet room cleared, closed, and confirmed with IT at convention close *(Ch. 11, 13)*
- [ ] Director thanks staff individually before they leave *(Ch. 13, 14)*

### Post-Convention

- [ ] Director reviews full accessibility log before debrief *(Ch. 14)*
- [ ] Hold post-con debrief with full team *(Ch. 3, 14)*
- [ ] Document debrief outcomes — observations, decisions, recommendations with owners and target dates *(Ch. 14)*
- [ ] Submit recommendations to convention leadership promptly *(Ch. 3, 14)*
- [ ] Update this framework with any generalizable lessons learned *(Ch. 14)*
- [ ] Update convention-specific configuration with any changed details *(Ch. 14)*
- [ ] Personally acknowledge each staff member's contributions *(Ch. 14)*
- [ ] Confirm Director and Assistant Director availability for next convention *(Ch. 3, 14)*
- [ ] Store post-con documentation where next year's planning cycle can find it *(Ch. 14)*

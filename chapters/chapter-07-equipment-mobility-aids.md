## Chapter 7: Equipment & Mobility Aids

*The accessibility department is responsible for sourcing, maintaining, and managing equipment that supports attendee participation. Equipment needs should be assessed during the venue assessment and confirmed during pre-con planning. All equipment decisions should be documented in the convention-specific configuration.*

---

### Types of Equipment

#### Mobility Aids
Mobility aids allow attendees who have difficulty walking or navigating large spaces to participate fully in the convention. The following types of aids are worth considering based on budget and anticipated need:

- **Wheelchairs** — standard manual wheelchairs for attendee loan during the convention
- **Transport chairs** — lighter, more compact than standard wheelchairs, pushed by an attendant rather than self-propelled
- **Walkers & rollators** — for attendees who need walking support but not full wheelchair assistance
- **Canes** — a small supply of canes may be useful for attendees who arrive without one and need temporary support
- **Mobility scooters** — powered scooters for attendees who cannot self-propel; higher cost and storage requirements than manual aids

The convention should determine which aids to provide based on budget, storage capacity, and anticipated attendee needs. At minimum, at least one wheelchair or transport chair is strongly recommended.

---

#### Sensory Support Equipment
Sensory support items help attendees with sensory processing sensitivities manage stimulation during the convention. These are typically stored at the accessibility desk or made available in a designated area.

Common items include:
- Fidget toys and sensory tools
- Noise-canceling earmuffs or earplugs
- Sunglasses or tinted lenses for light sensitivity
- Weighted lap pads

*Note: Quiet room specific equipment and furnishings are addressed in Chapter 11: Quiet Room.*

---

### Ownership Models

Conventions have two primary options for providing mobility aids:

#### Convention-Owned Equipment
The convention purchases equipment outright. This is the most cost-effective long-term solution for conventions that plan to offer mobility aid loans annually.

Considerations:
- Upfront purchase cost
- Storage between conventions
- Maintenance and inspection between uses
- Transport to and from the convention venue

#### Vendor Rental
The convention rents equipment from a local medical supply vendor for the duration of the event. This avoids storage and maintenance responsibilities but typically costs more per event.

Considerations:
- Research local vendors well in advance
- Confirm availability for convention dates
- Confirm delivery, pickup, and on-site storage logistics with the venue

---

### Shared Equipment Between Conventions

Where two or more conventions have a cooperative relationship, shared equipment ownership is worth considering. This model allows purchase costs to be shared and amortized across multiple events, reducing the financial burden on any single convention.

Practical considerations for shared equipment:
- Establish clearly which convention is the primary owner and responsible for storage
- Coordinate equipment transport around existing travel between conventions — equipment moves when people are already making the trip rather than requiring dedicated transport
- Inspect and service equipment after each use before it is stored or transferred
- Document the shared ownership arrangement formally so it survives leadership changes at either convention

*Note: Storage space is often at a premium for conventions. Shared ownership works best when at least one convention has reliable, accessible storage available between events.*

---

### Liability & Waivers

Loaning mobility aids to attendees introduces potential liability considerations. The convention should consult with its legal or insurance contacts to determine:
- Whether a liability waiver is required for mobility aid loans
- Whether existing convention insurance covers mobility aid loan incidents
- What inspection and maintenance standards are required

This is an open item for most conventions implementing mobility aid loans for the first time and should be resolved before equipment is made available to attendees.

---

### Equipment Inspection & Maintenance

All equipment should be inspected before each convention and after each use. Any damaged or unsafe equipment should be removed from service immediately. Establish a simple inspection checklist for each type of equipment and assign responsibility for inspection to a specific staff member.

---

### Documentation

Maintain a running inventory of all accessibility equipment including:
- Item description and quantity
- Purchase date and cost
- Current storage location
- Condition notes
- Shared ownership details if applicable

This inventory should be stored in the convention-specific configuration and updated after each event.

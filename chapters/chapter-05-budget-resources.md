## Chapter 5: Budget & Resources

*Accessibility departments vary widely in size, scope, and available funding. This chapter defines the categories of spending a director should plan for and provides guidance on building a budget request. Actual figures will vary by convention and should be documented in the convention-specific configuration. The most important principle: request your budget early. Several accessibility services have long lead times and cannot be secured without confirmed funding.*

---

### Budget Categories

#### Interpreter Services
ASL interpreter costs vary significantly depending on the number of interpreters required, the number of rooms or events to be covered, and the length of the convention. Costs are typically calculated per interpreter per hour or per day. Budget should account for:
- Interpreter fees
- Any required breaks or interpreter rotation (interpreters typically cannot work continuously for extended periods)
- Support volunteer discretionary budget for interpreter comfort (water, snacks, meals)

Interpreter services require a minimum of 6 months lead time to source and contract. Budget should be requested and confirmed no later than 9 months before the convention. *(see Chapter 6: Interpreter Services)*

---

#### Equipment & Mobility Aids
Costs depend on whether the convention owns equipment or rents from a vendor. Categories include:
- Mobility aids (wheelchairs, transport chairs, scooters)
- Sensory toys and quiet room supplies
- Storage and transport of owned equipment between conventions

Where multiple conventions share resources, purchase costs may be shared and amortized across events. *(see Chapter 7: Equipment & Mobility Aids)*

---

#### Printed Materials & Signage
Printed materials may include accessibility guides, venue maps, signage for the accessibility desk, quiet room, and accessible seating areas. Costs vary depending on quantity, quality, and whether printing is handled in-house or by a vendor. Budget should be revisited each year as needs and venue layouts change.

---

#### Badge Emblem System
Costs associated with producing and applying accessibility emblems to badges. May be minimal if handled through the convention's existing badge production process. Confirm with Registration whether this is a shared cost. *(see Chapter 9: Badge Emblem System)*

---

#### Supplemental Staff Recognition
Convention staff rewards (shirts, lanyards, badges, pins, etc.) are typically managed under the convention's general staff budget. However, the accessibility department may wish to maintain a small discretionary budget for recognizing staff who go above and beyond. This should be discussed with convention leadership during the budget request process.

---

#### Contingency
It is strongly recommended to request a small contingency allocation for unplanned needs identified during the convention. Accessibility encounters unpredictable situations by nature, and having flexibility to respond is valuable.

---

### Building Your Budget Request

Budget request processes vary by convention. At minimum, a request should include:
- A list of planned services and associated estimated costs
- Identification of any items requiring early funding commitment due to lead time
- Any carryover equipment or resources from previous years that reduce costs
- A brief summary of previous year spending if applicable

Work with convention leadership to understand the format, deadline, and approval process for budget requests at your specific convention.

### Timing

| Item | Request By |
|---|---|
| ASL Interpreter Services | 9+ months out |
| Mobility Aid Equipment/Rental | 6 months out |
| Printed Materials & Signage | 3 months out |
| Contingency & Miscellaneous | With initial budget request |

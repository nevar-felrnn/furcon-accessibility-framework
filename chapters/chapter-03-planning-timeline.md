## Chapter 3: Planning Timeline

*This chapter is your roadmap. You don't need to do everything at once — just follow the timeline and check in regularly. If you're starting late, see the sidebar below. Each milestone references the relevant chapter where full details, processes, and templates can be found. Use this chapter as your roadmap and return to it regularly to track progress.*

---
### 🚨 Starting Late?
If you inherited this role fewer than 6 months before the convention, don't panic. Here's your condensed priority list:
- **Month 1:** Confirm budget, start ASL sourcing, book quiet room
- **Month 2:** Begin staff recruitment, confirm desk location
- **Month 3:** Finalize training, confirm badge emblem system
- **Month 4:** At-con operations
*You may not achieve full coverage, but you can still make meaningful progress. Be transparent with attendees about what you can and cannot provide.*

### Before the Planning Cycle Begins
- Confirm Director and Assistant Director for the upcoming convention
- Ensure accessibility is represented in initial convention planning meetings
- Confirm board awareness of accessibility department needs
- Review post-con documentation from previous year *(see Chapter 14: Post-Con & Continuous Improvement)*

---

### 9+ Months Out
- Submit accessibility budget request to convention board *(see Chapter 5: Budget & Resources)*
- Begin ASL interpreter sourcing and contracting *(see Chapter 6: Interpreter Services)*
- Begin venue accessibility assessment *(see Chapter 4: Venue Assessment)*
- Identify and begin recruiting Assistant Director if not already in place

- Begin ASL interpreter sourcing and contracting *(see Chapter 6: Interpreter Services)*
**Note for Small Cons (<1,000 attendees):** 6 months may be sufficient for interpreter services. Focus on the main events room first, then expand as budget allows.

---

### 6 Months Out
- Confirm ASL interpreter contracts
- Confirm budget allocation from board
- Begin staff recruitment *(see Chapter 8: Staff Recruitment & Training)*
- Confirm quiet room location and IT coordination *(see Chapter 11: Quiet Room)*
- Begin cross-department coordination conversations with Safety, IT, and Registration *(see Chapter 12: Cross-Department Coordination)*

---

### 3 Months Out
- Staff recruitment target met
- Begin staff training *(see Chapter 8: Staff Recruitment & Training)*
- Confirm mobility aid plan — owned equipment or vendor rental *(see Chapter 7: Equipment & Mobility Aids)*
- Confirm accessibility desk location *(see Chapter 10: Accessibility Desk Operations)*
- Confirm badge emblem system with Registration *(see Chapter 9: Badge Emblem System)*

---

### 30 Days Out
- Final cross-department coordination with Safety, IT, and Registration
- Confirm volunteer roster and assignments
- Confirm all equipment is sourced and accessible
- Confirm quiet room setup with IT
- Distribute staff schedule and shift assignments
- Ensure all staff have reviewed training materials

---

### At Convention
- Accessibility desk operational during core convention hours
- Daily staff briefings at start of each shift
- Document all attendee interactions and unmet needs
- Maintain open communication channel with Safety, IT, and Registration
- Director available for consultation outside core hours
*(see Chapter 13: At-Con Operations)*

---

### Post-Convention
- Conduct post-con review with full team
- Document lessons learned and unmet needs
- Update framework and convention-specific documents
- Submit recommendations to board for following year
- Begin next planning cycle
*(see Chapter 14: Post-Con & Continuous Improvement)*

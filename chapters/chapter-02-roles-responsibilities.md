## Chapter 2: Roles & Responsibilities

### Accessibility Director / Lead

The Accessibility Director is the department head, responsible for the planning, staffing, and execution of all accessibility services. The Director reports to convention leadership and coordinates with other departments including Safety, IT, and Registration.

Core responsibilities include:
- Recruiting, training, and managing accessibility staff and volunteers
- Ensuring all staff understand their roles and the department's mission
- Overseeing pre-con planning milestones
- Serving as the primary point of contact for cross-department coordination
- Being available for consultation during convention hours and reachable after hours for urgent matters
- Leading post-con review and documentation for continuous improvement

---

### Assistant Director / Assistant Lead

The Assistant Director serves as the deputy to the Accessibility Director, acting as backup and stepping into the Director role when needed. This role may additionally own specific departmental functions such as staff training or documentation, making it a natural fit for someone with strong organizational or instructional skills.

Core responsibilities include:
- Supporting the Director across all planning and at-con operations
- Assuming Director responsibilities in the Director's absence
- Optionally owning staff training programs and/or department documentation
- Maintaining familiarity with all department functions to ensure seamless coverage

---

### Staff

Staff are experienced, committed volunteers who dedicate 20 or more hours over the course of the convention to the accessibility department. They are expected to have a deeper knowledge of department processes and to operate with a higher degree of independence than general volunteers. Staff are typically recognized with convention-specific rewards such as staff shirts, sweatshirts, lanyards, badges, pins, or other items as determined by convention leadership.

Core responsibilities include:
- Staffing the accessibility desk during assigned shifts
- Proactively identifying attendees who may need support
- Conducting attendee intake and documenting needs and outcomes
- Supporting volunteers and providing guidance as needed
- Escalating issues to the Assistant Director or Director when appropriate

---

### Volunteers

Volunteers provide flexible support capacity for the accessibility department, typically contributing a few hours per day or a set number of hours over the weekend. While volunteers may have less experience than staff, they play an important role in extending the department's reach. Volunteer engagement and hours may vary based on convention incentive structures.

Core responsibilities include:
- Supporting staff at the accessibility desk
- Assisting attendees with directions, equipment, or general needs
- Proactively and warmly approaching attendees who may benefit from support
- Reporting any concerns or unmet needs to staff or the Director

---

*Note: The roles defined above represent the current structure. As the department grows and needs evolve, additional specialized roles may be introduced. Role assignments should be revisited after each convention as part of the post-con review process.*
